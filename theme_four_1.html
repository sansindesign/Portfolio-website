For this assignment we were assigned teams and had to together come up with a new Fronter. We chose the students as our main focus group and designed the website according to their needs since thatâ€™s the group that uses it the most.
The first part of the assignment we did together as group, which consisted on mapping out the shortcoming of the current Fronter, coming up with a user journey for our prototype and also adding extra features to Fronter. Our user journey needed us to add the personal messaging feature to Fronter, which would make it a better platform for having conversations on-line with your classmates. In addition, we chose to cut down on the number of subpages Fronter has and make the important things as accessible as possible.

The actual design of the website was individual, but we had to follow the official KEA design guide. Following the guideline, I chose the red from the KEA logo as my main color for the website. My UI design approach kept things modern and intuitive. I brought all the necessary info the students look for on the home page and left out the unnecessary. For things with secondary importance, I designed buttons to make access to those sites quick and easy as well.
